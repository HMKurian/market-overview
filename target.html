What to Look for in a Stock?





Earnings:A companies earnings report can tell you alot about a company and whether or not you should invest in them. An earnings report tells you their net profit after subtracting expenses,interest and taxes from their revenue. Earnings tell you the profitability of a company and are one of the major factors in determining a stocks price depending on whether the company revenue rose or declined. Investors look at the Earnings Per Share or EPS to calculate the P/E(price-to-earning-ratio) which equalts the price of a share of stock divided by the company's earnings-per-share it indidicates whether a stock at its current market price is expensive or cheap a lower P/E ratio means the stock is considered to have more value.

Market:Learning the marktet of a company is a key factor in determining a companies valuation by learning how a company earns revenue and how hard it is to imitate you can predict its potential future growth. If your able to predict the future of a stock or company you could potentially hit it big and earn a lot especially if the stock is currently undervalued.If youd rather trust an experts analysis you can try to go to stock predictors

Risks:

Before investing in a stock you should look at its beta. Beta is a measure of the voltaility or systematic risk of a stock compared to the market as a whole essentially its a statistic that measures the expected increase or decreasee of a stock in proportion to the stock markets movements. Stocks with betas greater then 1 are generally considered more risky investing in stocks with a high beta means you could potentially make a lot of money but could also lose alot it's a must to watch high beta stocks closely. However if you dont have the time to watch a stock closely you should invest in a defensive stock with a low beta meaning it doesnt react to other S&P 500 movements as much as others so your money is much safer however you wont make much in a short amount of time.

Charts:Knowing how to read stocks charts allows you to be able to see the history of a stock and the short term perofrmance or posible trends that are emerging. stock charts and trends allow you to understand how well the stock is doing and gives you an idea of how well a stock will likely do in the short and long terms.

Dividends:Before investing you should look if the stock pays dividends which essentially offers an additional payout on top of the potential price. You should be wary of stocks that have yields that seem to high this is generally used to attract investors to a company that is struggling and may only be temporary. If your looking for a long term company invest in look for dividend aristocrats they are some of the most stable dividend payers in the stock market however these stocks are usually have a slowth growth for more info on dividends view the resources.
